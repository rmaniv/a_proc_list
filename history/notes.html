9. Savarkar and the Two-nation Theory (August 24, 2022)

Although Savarkar said that "[he had] no quarrel with Mr. Jinnah’a two nation theory. We, Hindus, are a nation by ourselves and it is a historical fact that Hindus and Muslims are two nations", he further added that (paraphrasing) Hindu Mahasabha was always willing to extend its hand of co-operaiion to all who accept the four principles, namely, territorial integrity of India, majority rule in provinces and the Centre, residuary powers in the Centre and recruitment to public services on merit alone. The second-part of the quote is often missed by lefties who want to portray Savarkar as supporting Partition.

From Mitra, H. N., Ed. The Indian Annual Register. Vol 2. 1943. p. 10. https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.45825/page/n27/mode/2up.

1. Did British or Gandhi's influences oust Bose from Congress Presidency?

It is pretty much well accepted that Mohandas Gandhi, Vallabhbhai Patel and members of the Congress Working Committee who were close to Gandhi worked to oust Subhas Bose after his historic win against Gandhi's supported Pattabhi Sitaramayya. But thanks to Adheer Som, I got introduced to this declassified CIA document which adds a complication to this generally accepted version of the event because it says that "[Subhas Chandra Bose,] although elected President Indian National Congress, 1938, was defeated by British influences in 1939." Why would CIA attribute something that Gandhi, Patel and others did openly to "British influences"? Wise men know that 2 + 2 = 4.

From CIA's OSS Note Cards. Declassified under the CREST Program, released on December 3, 2010. FOIA Document Number: CIA-RDP82-00038R001000150012-3. Electronic copy on CIA's online reading room can be found here (click).
