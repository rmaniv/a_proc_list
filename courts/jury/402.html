What are the qualities of dispute resolvers should have?

To resolve any dispute, the society needs to appoint “dispute-resolver” for each dispute. The dispute-resolver could be a single judge or a bench of judges or an officer (like Collector, Secretary) or a Jury (randomly chosen citizens) or some combination.

Now on many issues, such as determining facts, setting hearing dates and giving benefit of doubt, the written laws tend to leave the matter at the discretion of individuals who are resolving the disputes. Also, the dispute-resolvers may interpret the laws as they seem fit. So once the dispute-resolvers are determined, the speed and fairness of the court will depend less on the text of the law, but will depend more on the nature of dispute-resolvers. As a saying (maxim) goes, “Equity lies in individuals” i.e. fairness is in individuals (judges or Juries) who are giving judgements, not the law.

So what are the qualities that dispute-resolvers should have to ensure fairness and speed?

The dispute-resolvers should be skilled, intelligent and have knowledge of laws. But many (such as myself) believe that 2 factors are 1000 times more important than skill, intelligence and knowledge. These 2 factors are i)dispute resolvers must not have nexuses with eighter parties or their lawyers and they must not be partial ii)dispute-resolvers must have intense Hatred against criminal and corrupt.

      Summarizing - 1) what makes a very fair individual an unfair judge in judge system is that every day he has to attend 10-15 cases 2)when the case comes for next hearings after 2-3 months, the details have gone out of mind. 3)also, the judge simply does not have enough time to spare. 4)while in Jury System, the Jurors attend the case continuously without getting disrupted by other cases. This improves the speed as well as fairness.

    6. Bribing dispute-resolvers to give more and more dates is difficult in Jury System

      In case of Jury, the party which is interested in causing the delay will have to bribe at least 5 Jurors, again and again, everyday. It is very much possible to do so. But here lies the risk - a juror can record or videotape the exchange of bribe and then blackmail that party to pay huge sum. The Juror, since he has only chance to take bribe, if he is willing to take bribe, will try his best to set a trap. Once 1 or 2 such cases happen, the lawyers will start refraining from giving bribes.

    7. It will be easier/fairer to resolving pending 3 crore cases using Jury System

      Lets examine the issue of speed with some numerical details, with respect to existing situation of India

      Today there are over 3 crore cases pending in India. Lets say that it would take 16 hours for a judge to resolve a case on an average. Say the Jurors will be 5 times slower i.e. it would take 80 hours for a Jury to resolve the same “average” case.

      Number of days a judge would take to resolve a case = 2 Number of days a judge would work in a year = 250

      Number of cases a judge can resolve in a year = 250/2 = 125 Number of judges in India = 10,000 (approx.)

      Number of years 10000 judges can resolve in a year = 125 * 10000 = 12,50,000 Number of years needed to resolve 3 cr cases = 300,00,000/1250,000 = 24 yr


      So it would take over 24 years for judges to resolve the cases, assuming no new cases are filed, no appeals and that average case will take 2 days. If even 5% of cases go to appeals, the time span can increase to 50-100 years.

      What would it take to resolve the cases in 1 year using judge system? To resolve all the cases in 1 year, India would need to hire 240,000 judges. And 240,000 courtrooms. Each judge must be given a salary and allowances of Rs 30000 or else over 90% of the judges will start taking bribes. In addition, the government will need to provide each judge a car, a bungalow, a maali, a driver, a peon, free phone calls from office and home, medical care for entire family, security, etc. This will add up to over Rs 100,000 a month.

      The worst problem is - once these 240,000 judges are hired and say they resolve the pending 3 crore cases in 1-2 years, what will they do with once the cases are over? If these judges are to be fired after the workload is over, the anticipation that they are likely to be fired after 1-2 years will cause extreme insecurity amongst the judges, and this insecurity will result into more corruption. So if fewer judges are hired, so that none need to be fired, the delay will increase.

      Now lets see the situation in Jury System. With the money spent on salaries, bungalows, cars, drivers etc. of 240,000 judges in 6 months, it would possible to make 20,00,000 rooms to conduct Jury Trials. Say each Jury Trial takes 2 weeks. If 20,00,000 trials are conducted in 20,00,000 Jury rooms, each month 40,00,000 cases can be resolved. Thus in less than 1 year all the pending cases can be resolved. And once resolved, no judges need to be expelled.
